Surface 1

Ambient Visualization — Geometric Ocean

Geometric shapes (trait creatures) drift through the ocean layer alongside bubbles. Responds to conversational energy — not personality dimensions. Each trait has its shape: Circle (O), Half-Circle (C), Rectangle (E), Triangle (A), Diamond (N).

Act 1: Settling In

Gentle, slow. Few bubbles drift lazily. Shapes barely visible, distant. The ocean is calm — inviting the user in.

Act 2: Deep Exploration

Energy builds. More bubbles, faster. Shapes emerge more clearly, drifting closer. The ocean mirrors engagement depth.

Act 3: Convergence

Peak energy. Rich, active. Multiple bubble streams. Shapes prominent and closer. Deep caustic movement.

The Breath

Consolidation. Bubbles slow. Shapes drift to center. Caustics settle. 3-5 seconds of stillness.

Results / Portrait

Quiet background. Minimal motion. Shapes still present but faint. The room has gotten quieter.

🤿 The Dive Log

What a fascinating dive we just had. You found your rhythm quickly once we got past the surface — no hesitation when the questions went deeper. We covered some real territory together. What I see is someone who lives in an interesting contradiction: you crave novelty and unpredictability, but only when you feel prepared for it. You're emotionally deep but learning to trust that depth.

🧬 What Sets You Apart
What makes you, you

Even after a thousand dives in my log, this particular combination caught my attention. You have this selective relationship with uncertainty that I don't see often. Most people either love unpredictability or they don't. But you? You actively seek it in some areas and freeze up in others. You want the waves to surprise you, you want technology to reshape how you think — but when a stranger approaches you, uncertainty makes you shut down. That's not contradictory, it's strategic.

💎 Your Depths
What you're good at

Your ability to adapt in real-time is genuine. Once you're in the moment — on a wave, in an unpredictable situation you've prepared for — you thrive. That combination of preparation and flexibility is powerful.

You think in systems. When you talked about innovation and technology reshaping how we think — you see the bigger picture. You understand why things work the way they do, not just how.

🌊 Undercurrents
What limits you

Uncertainty paralysis with people is holding you back more than you realize. You know this — you can see it happening in real time when someone approaches you and you freeze. But here's what you might not see: that response is costing you connections with exactly the kind of people you want to meet.

⚓ The Anchor
What's holding you in place

There's a belief running underneath everything — that showing up authentically with people is inherently risky. You can handle uncertainty in waves, in technology, in ideas — but with people, your default is to protect first, connect second. What would it look like if you treated meeting new people like you treat reading the waves — observing first, then committing fully?

Why I built big-ocean

I took every personality test out there. 16Personalities. Truity. The OCEAN quizzes. Each time I'd get a result — four letters, a percentage, maybe a paragraph that could describe anyone. I'd read it, nod, and forget it within a day.

Then one day, a friend described me in a way I'd never heard before. Not a label — a story. She told me about the contradiction she saw: how I chase depth in everything but build my life to move fast. How I care deeply about people but always keep one foot near the exit. It stuck with me for weeks. Not because it was flattering — because it was true in a way no test result had ever been.

That's when I realized: the problem isn't the science. The Big Five model works. The problem is that no one turns the science into something that moves you. Something you remember.

So I built Nerin — an AI that doesn't just assess you, but gets to know you through real conversation. And then writes you something personal. Not a score. Not a type. A portrait.

Here's what Nerin wrote for me
"You chase depth in everything, but you've built a life that moves fast. There's a paradox you don't talk about — how the thing you want most (time to think) is the thing you sacrifice first. The projects you're most proud of aren't the ones that succeeded. They're the ones where you felt you understood something new about yourself while building them..."

🤿 Two Dives, One Ocean

There's a pattern I noticed across both your conversations that neither of you mentioned directly. Vincent described how Sarah "makes the room warmer," and Sarah described how Vincent "makes the plan clearer" — neither of you realized you were describing the same dynamic from opposite sides.

🧬 Where You Click
The frequencies you share

You operate on complementary frequencies. Vincent's conscientiousness (+34 above Sarah's) creates the scaffolding that Sarah's spontaneity fills with life. Sarah's extraversion (+34 above Vincent's) opens doors to people and experiences that Vincent's depth then makes meaningful. This isn't balance by accident — it's the reason you work.

Both of you scored high in openness — you share an intellectual curiosity and a love for exploring ideas together. When Vincent mentioned building systems "that make you think differently," Sarah lit up talking about "finding people who see the world sideways." Same impulse, different expression.

🌊 The Undercurrent
Where friction lives

The tension point to watch: Sarah processes out loud, Vincent processes internally. When Sarah needs to talk through a problem, Vincent's first instinct is to listen and solve. What Sarah actually wants is to be heard thinking, not fixed. When Vincent goes quiet, Sarah's first instinct is to fill the silence. What Vincent actually needs is space to arrive at his answer.

The 34-point conscientiousness gap shows up in how you plan together. Vincent wants the plan before the action. Sarah wants to start moving and figure it out on the way. Neither approach is wrong — but when one of you feels rushed and the other feels stalled, this is why.

⚓ What Holds You Together

Here's what I think you both already know but haven't said out loud: you make each other braver. Vincent takes social risks he wouldn't take alone because Sarah makes the room feel safe enough to try. Sarah slows down and goes deeper because Vincent's attention tells her that depth is worth the patience. You don't complete each other — you expand each other.

Chart & Data Visualization Language

Unified visual language for all data displays across Results, Public Profile, and Relationship Analysis. Components are reused — only the data and context changes.

Color as Identity

Every trait has a fixed color + shape pair. These never change across contexts. The user learns to read their personality through color alone.

O C E A N

Typography in Data

Scores, labels, and axis values always use --font-data (JetBrains Mono). Section titles use --font-heading (Space Grotesk). Descriptions use --font-body (DM Sans).

82/120

Grid Lines & Axes

Minimal. Grid rings on radar use --border at 0.5-1px. No fill on grid shapes. Progress bars use 6px height with 3px radius. Facet bars use 3px height. Background always --border.

Fill & Opacity

Radar polygon: trait-colored stroke (1.5px) + 12% opacity fill. Second person overlay: dashed stroke (6 3) + 10% fill. Confidence ring: 8px stroke, rounded caps. Progress bars: solid trait color. Never use gradients inside charts.

Vertex Markers

Radar vertices use filled circles (r=4) in the trait's color. In future: replace circles with the trait's geometric shape as vertex markers (circle for O, half-circle for C, etc.) for even stronger identity.

Animation

Charts animate on reveal only (not loops). Radar polygon draws from center outward (300ms ease-out). Progress bars fill left-to-right (400ms staggered 50ms per trait). Confidence ring draws clockwise. All respect prefers-reduced-motion.

Theme Behavior

Charts adapt through CSS custom properties only — no conditional rendering. The same SVG works in both themes because all colors reference var(--trait-*) and var(--border).

Light mode
  • Grid: #FFD6C4 (warm border)
  • Trait colors: vibrant (full saturation)
  • Fill: 12% opacity (cream shows through)
  • Card bg: #FFF0E8
Dark mode
  • Grid: #252A52 (deep border)
  • Trait colors: lighter variants (readable on dark)
  • Fill: 12% opacity (abyss shows through)
  • Card bg: #141838
