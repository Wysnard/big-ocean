## Story Information

**Story Type:** Empathy Story (emotional)

**Framework Used:** Empathy Story — primary frame: make the visitor feel *seen* and understood before asking them to act. Secondary lens: Character Arc — the visitor's relationship with self-knowledge evolves as they scroll, from guarded to curious to wanting.

**Purpose:** Landing page narrative — convince visitors to start a conversation with Nerin. The page itself IS the story, presented as a three-voice conversation: Nerin (chat bubbles), the visitor (lighter/smaller reactive bubbles), and a narrator (interface text for context and framing).

**Target Audience:**
- **Social media arrivals** — curious, short attention span, came from a hook/clip/share
- **Partner-referred users** — someone they know shared a relationship comparison; they arrive with social proof but no product context
- **Cold Google searchers** — landed from "personality test" or "Big Five" queries; comparing against incumbents (16Personalities, etc.)

**Key Messages:**
- Recognition happened for the founder — it might happen for you too (testimony, not promise)
- This is a conversation, not a quiz
- The output is a portrait, not a type
- Free, private, ~25 minutes, no wrong answers
- Social comparison (relationship overlay) as sharing hook

**Narrative Principles (refined from discussion):**
- **Nerin leads the page** — in her real voice (warm, direct, curious, grounded, speaks from experience, never clinical or flattering)
- **Vincent's story is present but not predominant** — one proof moment, not the frame
- **No personality-test baggage assumed** — entry point is broader, more human; some visitors have never tested, some are fine with labels
- **Recognition is the message, not a page experience** — the page conveys "this happened to me" and leaves the door open
- **The user voice is flexible** — can shift across the scroll (curious, guarded, wanting)
- **Structure is fully open** — redesign from scratch

**Constraints:**
- Scrollable chat-style conversation format
- Must serve all three audience segments without branching
- Nerin's voice must match her character bible (see `packages/domain/src/constants/nerin-persona.ts`)

**Design Constraints (from pre-mortem risk analysis):**

1. **Portrait needs one-beat setup.** Don't show the excerpt in a vacuum. Minimum frame: "After a 30-minute conversation, this is what Nerin wrote." Without this, it reads like copywriting, not proof.
2. **Hero must anchor AND differentiate.** Cold-start users expect a quiz. The hero needs "personality test" language (so they know they're in the right place) AND immediate signal that this is different ("not a quiz — a conversation"). Both in the first 3 seconds.
3. **Hero must create scroll momentum.** Don't resolve everything above the fold. Create an open loop — a question, a promise, a tension that can only be resolved by scrolling down. The best content is below; the hero should make that obvious.
4. **Pivot from third-person to second-person.** After showing Vincent's portrait, turn the lens onto the reader. Not "yours could be like this" (sales claim). Something that makes them think about their own patterns. "I don't know your version yet. But you have one."
5. **Brief credibility beat for skeptics.** The portrait alone doesn't prove provenance to technical users. One line: "Built on the Big Five — the framework psychiatrists actually use." Or a glimpse of the data layer (OCEAN scores, 30 facets) alongside the prose.
6. **Mobile-aware user voice.** User bubbles are lighter background, smaller text, less padding than Nerin's — prevents "I didn't say that" uncanny valley on mobile. Content is reactive fragments, not speeches.

## Story Structure

### Narrative Spine (Reverse-Engineered from Conversion Moment)

The page follows a 6-layer conversion chain, reordered based on user persona focus group findings. **Proof moves up to position 2** — the portrait excerpt is the strongest conversion asset and also *is* the connection with Nerin.

| Layer | What happens | What it gives the visitor |
|-------|-------------|--------------------------|
| **0. Attention** | First 3 seconds: light, fun-curious, unexpected | Reason to scroll |
| **1. Self-recognition** | One Nerin observation that names something universal | "This gets me" |
| **2. Proof** | Portrait excerpt shown early — concrete, not theoretical | "This is real" + "I'd talk to her" |
| **3. Permission** | Dissolve fears: judgment, privacy, time, depth, boredom | "I can do this" |
| **4. Action** | CTA — start the conversation | Click |

**Key design principles:**
- **Proof before explanation.** Show the output, then they'll care about the method. Don't build up to the portrait — lead with it.
- **Self-recognition comes before product explanation.** The visitor needs to feel something familiar about themselves before they care about how the tool works.
- **The portrait excerpt IS the connection with Nerin.** It demonstrates her voice better than any description could. One sharp observation beats ten warm ones.
- **Design for cold-start.** If a zero-context Google searcher gets it, everyone does.

**First Principles (what the conversation format uniquely enables):**

1. **The page IS a micro-version of the product.** Not a description of a conversation — the beginning of one. Nerin on the landing page *is* Nerin, not her marketing copy. The visitor is already in a conversation before they click anything.
2. **Feeling before understanding.** Skip the explanation. Nerin names something on the page — something small, universal, precisely observed — and the visitor feels what it's like to be seen by her. That's a free sample, not a product description.
3. **CTA = "keep going," not "start."** The page blurs the boundary between reading about the product and being inside it. The last Nerin message should feel mid-conversation. The CTA continues the thread, not begins it. Consider: "Tell me" / "I'm ready" / "Let's find out" instead of "Start your conversation."
4. **Nerin's voice IS the social proof.** Every Nerin message on the page is a live demonstration. If the visitor thinks "she's sharp" or "that's not how chatbots talk" — that's more convincing than any testimonial carousel.
5. **Demonstrate, don't argue.** If Nerin is sharp, you don't need to argue AI can read people. If the portrait is specific, you don't need to argue results aren't generic. If the page is engaging, you don't need to promise it won't be boring. Only privacy and fear of judgment need explicit handling — everything else dissolves through the experience.

### Opening Hook (Hero Section)

**Metaphor:** The dive shop. Nerin is the divemaster, talking inside the shop. The visitor overhears something that makes them stop. They look up, see the sign (narrator), and step inside (scroll).

**Hero layout:**
1. **Brand mark** (narrator) — "big-ocean" + OCEAN shapes
2. **Nerin bubble** — the overheard fragment: **"What if the most interesting person in the room is you?"**
3. **Narrator context** — grounding: personality portrait · conversation · 25 min · free
4. **CTA** (narrator space) — the door into the shop

**Why this line works:** Light, curious, flattering-without-flattery ("what if"), fun. Creates curiosity about yourself. Works for all three personas: Léa smiles, Marcus is intrigued, Sophie thinks "that's different." No test baggage. No heaviness. Pure Layer 0: intrigue + lightness.

**The hero IS the first beat of the conversation.** No seam between "marketing page" and "conversation." The Nerin bubble in the hero is the first bubble of the conversation flow. Scrolling = stepping inside.

### Key Story Beats

**Raw Story Elements (gathered from founder + Socratic elicitation):**

**Layer 0 — Attention:**
- Design for cold-start Google user with zero context. If they get it, everyone does.
- Hook cocktail: fun + curiosity + "I can't get this elsewhere"
- Tone: light, not clinical, not heavy
- **The dinner party pitch IS the page:** "It's a personality test but you talk to an AI called Nerin for 30 min, she writes you a portrait — like a letter tailored to your story." That natural explanation should be the energy of the page.
- **Format itself is the hook:** visitor expects a quiz → sees a conversation instead. Must land as intrigue, not confusion. The conversational format is the brand — people remember "that site where it was a conversation."

**Layer 1 — Self-recognition:**
- The format IS the recognition: conversation + free-form portrait. That's the thing no other personality test does. A test says what you are. A portrait shows how you work.
- Not a statement about tests. Not an argument. The visitor recognizes the differentiation *in the format itself* — "this isn't a quiz, this is actually talking to me."
- The universal truth doesn't need to be about personality specifically — it's about the experience of being *seen* by another person. Personality is the vehicle.
- Must work for people who've never taken a personality test AND people who've taken 20.

**Layer 2 — Proof (Vincent's portrait moment):**
- Core example: Nerin identified Vincent's problem-solving pattern — outward it's a superpower (career, hobbies, managing situations), inward it becomes self-punishment (replaying mistakes for days, finding the weak link which turns out to be his own vulnerabilities)
- This is NOT a label ("you're conscientious"). It's a *mechanism* — a pattern that runs through his entire life, named from the outside for the first time.
- **Must be framed as personal, not universal.** A portrait that could apply to everyone feels generic and kills the impressiveness. The specificity is what makes people go quiet and lean in.
- **Needs conversation context to land.** The portrait is impressive *because* it came from a 30-min conversation with an AI — not 6 months of therapy. Without that framing, the visitor won't understand why it's remarkable.
- **What happens when you tell people:** They go quiet. They don't say much. But they start asking more questions. They're invested. They wonder "what about me?" — that's the conversion moment.

**Layer 3 — Permission (fear inventory):**

| Fear | Core anxiety | Dissolution method |
|------|-------------|-------------------|
| "What if she tells me I'm a narcissist" | Fear of judgment | **Address explicitly** — Nerin doesn't judge, she observes |
| "My inner self used against me" | Privacy/trust | **Address explicitly** — private by default, you own it |
| "AI can't read human hearts" | Skepticism about the medium | **Dissolved by portrait excerpt** — the specificity kills this objection on contact |
| "30 min isn't enough to understand me" | Doubt about depth | **Dissolved by portrait excerpt** — proof of depth |
| "30 min is too long up front" | Commitment hesitation | **Dissolved by page experience** — if the page itself is engaging, 30 min sounds fine |
| "The conversation will be boring" | Fear of wasted time | **Dissolved by page experience** — if reading Nerin on the landing page is engaging, this fear disappears |

**Layer 4 — Relationship analysis (additional hook):**
- Frame as: caring about someone you love. Not "compare your scores."
- Works for partner-referred users AND cold-start users — even someone who came for self-discovery can be drawn in by "understand how you connect with the people you love"
- Emotional frame: this isn't just about you. It's about the space between you and someone who matters.

**Structural approach — Refined Hybrid: Three-Voice Conversation**

The page is a conversation with three distinct voices. Nerin leads. The user reacts. The narrator frames.

**Three voices:**

| Voice | Visual treatment | Role |
|-------|-----------------|------|
| **Nerin** | Full chat bubble, left-aligned, solid background, full-size text | Leads the page. Her real voice — warm, direct, grounded, speaks from experience. Every Nerin bubble is a live demo of the product. |
| **User** | Chat bubble, right-aligned, lighter background, **smaller text**, less padding | Reactive fragments. Short — lowercase energy, tentative, real. Things like: "...really?", "okay but how", "that could be anyone though", "...what would mine say?" Keeps the dialogue rhythm without competing with Nerin. |
| **Narrator** | No bubble. Muted typography (small caps, mono, or muted sans-serif). Interface-like. | Carries anything that would sound fabricated in either voice: context, logistics, framing, portrait setup, privacy statement, credibility beat, section markers. The narrator is the stage, not a character. |

**Why three voices:**
- Conversation format preserved (the brand — people remember "that site where it was a conversation")
- User bubbles maintain dialogue feel — the page looks like a chat, not a monologue
- Smaller/lighter user bubbles prevent "I didn't say that" uncanny valley on mobile
- Narrator protects the authenticity of both Nerin and user voices — anything that serves the PAGE goes to narrator, not into a character's mouth
- The editorial test: Would Nerin actually say this? → Nerin bubble. Would a visitor actually think this? → User bubble. Would it sound fake in either voice? → Narrator.

**Content calibration:**
- User messages are MINIMAL — reactions, not speeches. Fragments, not arguments.
- Nerin messages carry the substance. She is the voice in the room. The user is the whisper in response.
- Narrator interventions are sparse and quiet — signage, not commentary.
- **Vincent moment:** One beat. His real voice. Distinct visual treatment (a quote, a card, something personal). Not in a user bubble — this is the founder speaking, not the visitor.

**Reserved line for mid-page (when danger arrives):**
"You can live inside a pattern your whole life and never once see it." — Nerin bubble, deeper in the conversation, near the portrait excerpt. This is where the weight lands. Not at the door.

### Emotional Arc

**Core emotion: Dangerous curiosity** — "I can see this thing might actually see me. That's exciting. That's terrifying. And I want to do it anyway."

Three feelings braided together throughout the page:
- **Awe** — "this is more powerful than I expected"
- **Vulnerability** — "what if it sees something I don't want to see"
- **Desire** — "...but I want to know"

**Arc by scroll position:**

| Scroll position | Emotion | Trigger |
|----------------|---------|---------|
| **Hero** | Intrigue + lightness | "This isn't what I expected" — format, tone, curiosity |
| **Nerin's first observation** | Small recognition | She names something familiar — a human thing |
| **Portrait setup** | Lean-in | "After one conversation, Nerin wrote this about..." |
| **Portrait excerpt** | Awe + silence | The specificity. The mechanism. "An AI wrote *that*?" |
| **The pivot** | Dangerous curiosity | "I don't know your version yet." Fear + desire arrive together |
| **Permission beat** | Relief | Private. Free. No judgment. Fear acknowledged, not argued away |
| **CTA** | Wanting | "...I want to know" — desire wins, gently |

**Shape:** No valley. Steady build of warmth and intrigue → peaks at portrait (awe) → pivots into personal tension (dangerous curiosity) → releases into safety (permission) → resolves into wanting (CTA).

The one moment of discomfort — "what if it sees something I don't want to see" — is the *peak*, not a valley. It's when the page becomes real. And it lives right next to desire, not alone.

### Resolution/Call to Action

Sticky chat input bar — mimics the product UI. Input box + send button. "Start the conversation →". The visitor is already inside the experience. Clicking continues it, not starts it.

## Story Elements Analysis

### Character/Voice

**Nerin's voice (from character bible):**
- Warm but never soft. Calm, direct, genuinely curious.
- Speaks from experience: "I've seen this pattern enough times..."
- Confident without arrogant. Honest without harsh. Concise — every sentence earns its place.
- Grounded in plain language. Uses "we" for shared experience, "I" for observations.
- NEVER: clinical ("You exhibit high openness"), horoscope-like ("You have a deep inner world"), flattering ("That's amazing!"), hedging ("I might be wrong, but...")
- Pulls stories, not introspection. Relates first, reflects second.

**Vincent's voice:**
- Present but not predominant. Personal testimony, not product pitch.
- Shares what the experience *did* to him, not what the product *does*.

**User voice on page:**
- Flexible — can shift from curious to guarded to wanting across the scroll.
- Not locked to "skeptic." Represents the visitor's inner monologue.

### Conflict/Tension

- Internal: the visitor's tension between wanting to be known and fearing what they'd see
- External: every personality tool before this has reduced them, so why would this be different?
- The page doesn't resolve this tension through argument — it resolves it through demonstration (the portrait excerpt)

### Transformation/Change

- Visitor arrives: doesn't know what this is, or mildly curious, or following a friend's link
- Visitor leaves: wanting — "I wonder what mine would say"
- The shift happens because they felt something, not because they were convinced of something
