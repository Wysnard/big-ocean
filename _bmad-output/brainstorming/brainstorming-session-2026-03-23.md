# Brainstorming Session Results

**Facilitator:** Vincentlay
**Date:** 2026-03-23

## Session Overview

**Topic:** Homepage improvement — messaging, layout, and UX that communicates what Big Ocean is and motivates first-time visitors to start a personality assessment conversation.

**Goals:**
- Make the product instantly understandable to a new visitor
- Reduce friction and increase motivation to start an assessment conversation

### Session Setup

_Focus on the full homepage experience: hero messaging, value proposition clarity, social proof, CTA design, and the emotional/psychological triggers that move a curious visitor into action. Big Ocean is a conversational personality assessment built on the Big Five framework — the challenge is making that feel approachable and compelling, not academic._

## Technique Selection

**Approach:** AI-Recommended Techniques
**Analysis Context:** Homepage improvement with focus on clarity of value proposition and first-time visitor conversion

**Recommended Techniques:**

- **Role Playing:** Step into different visitor personas to understand what each type needs to see and feel on the homepage
- **Question Storming:** Generate the real questions first-time visitors have — revealing gaps the homepage must address
- **SCAMPER Method:** Systematically transform each homepage element using 7 creative lenses, informed by persona and question insights

**AI Rationale:** This sequence moves from empathy (understanding visitors) → gap discovery (what's missing) → concrete action (what to change). Each phase builds on the previous, ensuring improvements are grounded in real visitor needs rather than assumptions.

## Technique Execution Results

### Role Playing (Personas & Barriers)

**Interactive Focus:** Inhabiting different visitor personas to understand their experience of the homepage

**Key Ideas Generated:**

**[Persona #1]**: The Zero-Context Visitor
_Concept_: Someone arriving without prior knowledge reads "Not a personality quiz. A conversation." and has no frame for what this conversation is for. The headline defines by negation without stating the positive value.
_Novelty_: The hero assumes the visitor already knows they want a personality assessment.

**[UX #2]**: Dual-CTA Dilution
_Concept_: "Start your conversation" and "See how it works" compete for attention. The scroll-down CTA is redundant.
_Novelty_: The secondary CTA signals uncertainty which undermines the primary CTA.

**[Content #3]**: Beat 1 Doesn't Earn Attention
_Concept_: The opening Nerin message lacks a scannable hook. No bold heading. Users' eyes jump to Beat 3 ("That's Not a Bug") because it has a heading.
_Novelty_: Every chat bubble competes equally visually but they shouldn't. The first one needs to win instantly.

**[Content #4]**: User Bubbles Are Skippable
_Concept_: The fake "user" messages feel like filler to a real first-time visitor. They don't identify with a scripted user.
_Novelty_: The conversational format works for Nerin but simulated user responses may feel artificial.

**[Strategy #5]**: Missing "Why Should I Care?"
_Concept_: The page assumes the visitor is already on a self-understanding journey. It sells the method and the output but never answers: "Why does knowing myself better matter? What changes?"
_Novelty_: The homepage sells the HOW without first selling the WHY.

**[Persona #6]**: The Invited Friend
_Concept_: Someone's partner/friend sent them a link for the relationship analysis. They don't care about self-knowledge yet. The homepage doesn't acknowledge this entry point. They have to scroll through the entire self-discovery pitch before hitting the comparison section.
_Novelty_: Highest intent visitor, worst served by the current page.

**[Persona #7]**: The Social Media Curious
_Concept_: Saw a clip or someone sharing their OCEAN code. Coming in with "that looks fun." They want fast, low-friction, playful energy. The homepage tone is a mismatch.
_Novelty_: The fun-seeker needs permission to treat it lightly at first.

**[Persona #8]**: The Accidental Therapy-Seeker
_Concept_: Someone going through something who lands here thinking this might give them clarity. They're emotionally open but fragile. The skeptic tone may not resonate.
_Novelty_: This person doesn't need convincing about the method. They need to feel safe.

**[Strategy #9]**: The Missing Conversion Bridge
_Concept_: All different visitors can become people who value self-understanding but the homepage doesn't walk them across that bridge. It has one narrative arc (skeptic to convinced) but 4-5 different starting points.
_Novelty_: One funnel for a multi-door audience.

**[Strategy #10]**: The Test-Frame Trap
_Concept_: The entire homepage narrative is built around "we're better than other tests." If you haven't taken a test, this framing is irrelevant. Defining yourself against tests only matters to people who already think in terms of tests.
_Novelty_: The homepage is having a conversation about something most visitors never asked about.

**[Strategy #11]**: The Commitment Gap
_Concept_: Curiosity alone doesn't justify a 30-minute investment. There needs to be a promise of transformation or revelation — something you'll have after that you don't have now.
_Novelty_: Most personality tools convert on curiosity because they take 2 minutes. Big Ocean's longer format needs a deeper hook.

**[Strategy #12]**: Weight as Universal Hook
_Concept_: A slightly provocative, weighty opening line works across personas because it creates different reactions that all lead to engagement. The therapy-seeker thinks "yes, I need this." The fun-seeker thinks "ooh that's bold."
_Novelty_: The homepage doesn't need to be lighter. It needs to be sharp enough that even casual visitors stop.

**[Barrier #13]**: Value Not Visible Fast Enough
_Concept_: The homepage reveals the value proposition gradually through a long scroll. The most compelling proof is buried deep. The page rewards patience but punishes scanning.
_Novelty_: The homepage's greatest strength (immersive format) is also its conversion weakness.

**[Barrier #14]**: Value Too Abstract
_Concept_: "A portrait of who you are" — what does that mean? The visitor can't picture what they'll receive. The horoscope vs. portrait comparison is the first concrete glimpse and it's halfway down.
_Novelty_: The homepage describes the output but doesn't show it early enough.

**[Barrier #15]**: Process Fear
_Concept_: 25-30 minutes talking to an AI about yourself could sound like an awkward therapy session or tedious questionnaire. The homepage never shows what the conversation actually feels like.
_Novelty_: Addresses "different from tests" extensively but never addresses "actually enjoyable to do."

**[Strategy #16]**: The Founder Pitch Deconstruction
_Concept_: When Vincent pitches in person, he leads with a concrete transformation story — "the AI told me I turn my problem-solving against myself, and now I forgive myself more easily." The homepage sells the container. The pitch sells the content.
_Novelty_: The homepage currently doesn't use the approach that actually converts in real life.

**[Strategy #17]**: The One-Sentence Converter
_Concept_: Most people want to try it just from hearing "it's a personality test but instead of a quiz it's a conversation with an AI." One sentence + social trust = conversion.
_Novelty_: The homepage takes 14 beats to do what one sentence does in person.

**[Strategy #18]**: Transformation > Description
_Concept_: "It changed the way I move through the world" is infinitely more powerful than "you get a portrait with your OCEAN code and archetype." Nobody commits 30 minutes for a document. They commit for the chance one sentence changes how they see themselves.
_Novelty_: The homepage sells deliverables. The real sell is what those deliverables do to you.

**[Strategy #19]**: The Concrete Example Is the Hook
_Concept_: The problem-solving/self-forgiveness story communicates more than the entire horoscope vs. portrait comparison. It's specific, emotional, surprising, and instantly shows Nerin's depth.
_Novelty_: One real transformation story outperforms any feature explanation.

**[Strategy #20]**: People Don't Need Convincing — They Need Understanding
_Concept_: The concept itself is compelling. The 14-beat skeptic arc might be solving a problem most visitors don't have. Most arrive with curiosity that just needs a clear path forward, not a debate.
_Novelty_: The homepage is built to overcome objections. Most visitors don't arrive with objections.

### Question Storming (Visitor Questions & Fears)

**Interactive Focus:** Generating every question a first-time visitor might have, conscious or unconscious

**Key Questions Identified:**

_First 3 Seconds:_ What is this? Is this free? How long? What is Nerin? What do I get?
_First Scroll:_ Why is an AI talking to me in chat bubbles? Who is the "user" — am I that person? Is this about personality tests? Am I reading a demo or a sales pitch?
_Commitment Moment:_ Will this be awkward? Do I need an account? Can I stop halfway? What will it ask me?
_Differentiation:_ How is it different from other tests? From therapy? Can an AI understand the human heart?
_Fears:_ What if it shows me something I don't like (narcissist)? What if the relationship analysis destroys my couple? How vulnerable should I be?
_Practical:_ What do you do with my data? Can I extend the duration? 30 min is too long for something unproven.

**[Fear #17]**: Self-Exposure — "What if it says I'm a narcissist?"
_Concept_: Privacy from others is addressed on the page. Privacy from yourself is not. This is a completely different fear.

**[Fear #18]**: Relationship Damage — "What if we discover we're incompatible?"
_Concept_: The comparison feature is sold as positive but for some it's terrifying.

**[Tension #20]**: 30 min too short / too long paradox
_Concept_: Same number triggers opposite objections. The homepage presents 25 min as neutral fact rather than addressing either tension.

**[Question #21]**: Vulnerability Calibration — "How open should I be?"
_Concept_: People need permission to be honest with an AI and reassurance that honesty leads to better results.

**Post-Experience Insights (from friends/family):**

**[Question #29]**: Pricing Surprise — "I have to PAY to see my results?"
_Concept_: People invest 30 min of vulnerability then discover the portrait has a cost. Feels like a trap even though it's PWYW.

**[Credibility #31]**: "Is this just flattering me?"
_Concept_: People want to feel seen but also trust that what they're reading is true, not just nice.

**[Strategy #32]**: The Product Is Better Than the Marketing
_Concept_: Everyone was surprised by the quality. Visitors make decisions based on expectations far below what they'll actually receive.

**[Strategy #33]**: "Personally Written To Me" Is the Killer Line
_Concept_: The actual differentiator. Not science, not methodology. The feeling of "this was written about ME specifically."

**[Strategy #34]**: Nerin's Conversation Quality Is an Unplayed Card
_Concept_: Everyone is surprised by how good the conversation is. The homepage shows Nerin as a salesperson. The product shows Nerin as a deeply perceptive partner.

### Creative Facilitation Narrative

_This session revealed a fundamental misalignment between the homepage's narrative architecture and its actual visitor population. The homepage was built as a persuasion engine for test-skeptics, but most visitors either don't think about personality tests or arrive with entirely different motivations (invited for relationship analysis, social media curiosity, emotional searching). The breakthrough moment was when Vincent described his in-person pitch — a single transformation story that converts instantly — and realized the homepage doesn't use this approach at all. The product consistently exceeds expectations, meaning the marketing problem isn't overpromising — it's underselling._
