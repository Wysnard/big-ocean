The Science

Five traits. Thirty facets. One you.

The Big Five model is the most empirically validated framework in personality psychology, backed by decades of cross-cultural research.

But most tools only scratch the surface with 5 broad scores. Big Ocean goes six times deeper—mapping each trait across its 6 constituent facets to reveal the nuances that make your personality uniquely yours.

Openness

Your appetite for novelty, imagination, and intellectual exploration. High scorers are drawn to abstract ideas, artistic expression, and unconventional experiences. Low scorers prefer the practical, the concrete, and the familiar.

6 Facets

  • Imagination
  • Artistic Interests
  • Emotionality
  • Adventurousness
  • Intellect
  • Liberalism
Conscientiousness

Your relationship with structure, discipline, and long-term goals. This isn't just about being "organized"—it's about how you navigate the tension between ambition and spontaneity.

6 Facets

  • Self-Efficacy
  • Orderliness
  • Dutifulness
  • Achievement-Striving
  • Self-Discipline
  • Cautiousness
Extraversion

Your energy orientation—where you draw it from and how you spend it. Beyond "introvert vs extrovert," this reveals your relationship with excitement, assertiveness, and social warmth.

6 Facets

  • Friendliness
  • Gregariousness
  • Assertiveness
  • Activity Level
  • Excitement-Seeking
  • Cheerfulness
Agreeableness

How you balance your own needs with others'. This trait maps your capacity for trust, empathy, cooperation, and the strategies you use to navigate conflict and collaboration.

6 Facets

  • Trust
  • Morality
  • Altruism
  • Cooperation
  • Modesty
  • Sympathy
Neuroticism

Your emotional landscape—the intensity, frequency, and texture of your negative emotions. Not a weakness score. Understanding this trait is understanding your emotional operating system.

6 Facets

  • Anxiety
  • Anger
  • Depression
  • Self-Consciousness
  • Immoderation
  • Vulnerability
