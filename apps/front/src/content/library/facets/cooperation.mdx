## What cooperation measures

Cooperation captures your orientation toward social harmony — how strongly you prefer to avoid confrontation and how readily you seek common ground in disagreements. It reflects your comfort with friction versus your drive to smooth things over.

## When cooperation is more confrontational

People on the lower end stand their ground in disagreements, viewing healthy friction as a path to better outcomes. They do not back down just to keep the peace, and they believe that direct conflict — when managed well — produces stronger decisions than polite avoidance. This directness can feel uncomfortable but often surfaces issues that need airing.

## When cooperation is stronger

People on the higher end seek common ground instinctively, smoothing tensions so everyone walks away satisfied. They dislike confrontation and would rather compromise than win an argument. This harmony-seeking creates pleasant group dynamics and reduces interpersonal stress, though it may sometimes suppress important disagreements.

## How it shows up in daily life

Cooperation shapes how you handle conflict at work, at home, and in social settings. Higher scorers tend to mediate, accommodate, and de-escalate. Lower scorers tend to debate, push back, and hold firm. Teams need both: cooperators keep relationships intact, and challengers keep ideas honest.
