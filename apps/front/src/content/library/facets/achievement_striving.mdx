## What achievement striving measures

Achievement striving captures the intensity of your drive to set high standards, pursue goals, and measure progress. It reflects how much of your identity and satisfaction comes from accomplishment — not status, but the personal sense that you are moving toward something meaningful.

## When striving is more relaxed

People on the lower end take life at a comfortable pace, finding satisfaction in the journey rather than fixating on milestones. They can work hard when motivated, but ambition does not set the tempo of their daily life. Contentment comes from experiences, relationships, and engagement with the present — not from the next achievement on the list.

## When striving is intense

People on the higher end are fuelled by ambition. They set high standards, track progress, and push until the goal is reached. Rest feels earned only after a milestone is crossed. This drive produces impressive output and rapid growth, though it can also make relaxation feel like wasted time if not managed carefully.

## How it shows up in daily life

Achievement striving shows up in how you set goals and react to progress. Higher scorers keep to-do lists, celebrate completed milestones, and feel restless during idle periods. Lower scorers may skip the list entirely, working on what feels most alive in the moment and measuring a good day by how it felt rather than what got done. Both approaches sustain productive lives — they simply define "productive" differently.
