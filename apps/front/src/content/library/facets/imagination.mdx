## What imagination measures

Imagination captures how actively your mind generates internal scenarios, possibilities, and mental simulations that go beyond what is directly in front of you. In the NEO model it was originally labelled "Fantasy" — the tendency to create a rich, elaborate inner world. High scorers live partly in the realm of what-could-be; low scorers stay anchored in what-is.

## When imagination is quieter

People on the lower end tend to ground their thinking in concrete, observable reality. They prefer conversations that stick to facts, tested methods, and tangible outcomes. This is not a lack of creativity — it is a preference for ideas that have already earned their place through practical use. Decision-making feels most natural when supported by evidence rather than speculation.

## When imagination is vivid

People on the higher end drift naturally into possibility space. They picture alternative futures, rehearse conversations that haven't happened, and find that new ideas arrive as images or stories rather than bullet points. This rich inner life can fuel creative work, strategic foresight, and empathy — but can also pull attention away from immediate demands.

## How it shows up in daily life

Imagination influences the texture of thinking more than its speed. In meetings, high-imagination people may mentally simulate how a plan plays out before anyone finishes presenting it. In downtime, they are more likely to daydream productively — running mental experiments, exploring "what if" branches, or simply enjoying the play of ideas. Lower-imagination people tend to stay present, responding to what is happening now rather than what might happen later.
