## What anxiety measures

Anxiety captures the level of free-floating apprehension and worry you carry — how readily your mind flags potential threats, problems, and worst-case scenarios, even when the current situation is objectively fine.

## When anxiety is lower

People on the lower end face uncertainty with a calm, even-keeled mindset. Worry rarely takes hold, and they are not inclined to catastrophise. This composure lets them function well under pressure and make decisions without excessive second-guessing. They are the steady presence in a crisis.

## When anxiety is higher

People on the higher end stay alert to what could go wrong, channelling that vigilance into thorough preparation. Their worry is not irrational — it is anticipatory. They plan for contingencies, double-check important details, and often catch risks that calmer people miss. The cost is that this alertness can be tiring and hard to switch off.

## How it shows up in daily life

Anxiety influences how you prepare, how you sleep, and how you process uncertainty. Higher scorers may rehearse difficult conversations in advance, keep backup plans for backup plans, and feel tension in their body even when nothing is actively wrong. Lower scorers may wing it more confidently, sleep easily before big events, and spend less time on contingency planning. Both orientations contribute to resilient teams — vigilance prevents disasters, calm prevents paralysis.
