## What intellect measures

Intellect — called "Ideas" in the NEO model — captures your appetite for abstract thought, intellectual puzzles, and conceptual exploration. It is not a measure of intelligence; it is a measure of how much you enjoy thinking for its own sake.

## When intellectual curiosity is more focused

People on the lower end apply knowledge practically, zeroing in on what matters for the task at hand. They prefer depth in their domain over breadth across many topics. Ideas are evaluated by usefulness rather than elegance, and conversations that drift into pure abstraction may feel like a detour from the real point.

## When intellectual curiosity is broad

People on the higher end love exploring concepts, debating possibilities, and chasing ideas across disciplinary boundaries. A new framework, an unusual argument, or an unsolved problem can hold their attention for hours. Learning is intrinsically rewarding — they read, listen, and discuss not because they have to but because ideas light them up.

## How it shows up in daily life

Intellect influences what you reach for in spare moments. Higher scorers may gravitate toward documentaries, philosophy podcasts, or long-form essays, and they often steer casual conversations toward "big questions." Lower scorers may prefer hands-on activities, practical guides, and conversations that stay grounded in shared experience. Both styles contribute to teams and relationships — one generates new possibilities, the other grounds them in reality.
