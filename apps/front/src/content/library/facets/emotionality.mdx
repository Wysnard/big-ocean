## What emotionality measures

Emotionality — labelled "Feelings" in the NEO model — captures how readily you tune into your own inner emotional states and how much weight those feelings carry in your daily experience. It is about emotional receptiveness, not emotional stability or volatility.

## When emotionality is quieter

People on the lower end process emotions internally with a steady, analytical posture. They may experience strong feelings but tend to keep them below the surface, examining them logically before allowing them to influence behaviour. This composure can be an asset in high-pressure environments where clear-headed decisions matter most.

## When emotionality is more open

People on the higher end register their own emotions quickly and vividly. Joy, sadness, curiosity, and discomfort all arrive with clarity, giving them a rich internal compass for navigating relationships and decisions. This awareness fuels empathy and self-understanding, though it also means emotional events can feel more intense.

## How it shows up in daily life

Emotionality shapes how you relate to your own inner world. Higher scorers may keep a journal, seek out emotionally resonant music, or process a difficult conversation by naming what they felt. Lower scorers may prefer to move on, solve the problem, and reflect later — or not at all. Neither approach is better; they represent different strategies for managing the constant stream of inner experience.
