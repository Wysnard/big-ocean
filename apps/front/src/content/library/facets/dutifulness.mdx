<h2 id="what-dutifulness-measures">What dutifulness measures</h2>

Dutifulness captures how strongly a sense of moral obligation and responsibility guides your behaviour. It reflects the weight you give to promises, commitments, and the expectations others place on you — and how much discomfort you feel when those obligations go unfulfilled.

<h2 id="when-dutifulness-is-more-independent">When dutifulness is more independent</h2>

People on the lower end follow their own compass first. They honour commitments, but personal judgment takes priority over external expectations. If a rule feels arbitrary or a commitment no longer makes sense, they feel comfortable renegotiating rather than grinding through out of obligation alone.

<h2 id="when-dutifulness-is-strong">When dutifulness is strong</h2>

People on the higher end treat their word as a contract. Once they commit, follow-through is almost automatic — it would feel wrong to drop the ball. Responsibility and reliability are core to their self-image. This dependability earns trust but can also lead to overcommitment if they struggle to say no.

<h2 id="how-it-shows-up-in-daily-life">How it shows up in daily life</h2>

Dutifulness shapes how you handle promises, deadlines, and group expectations. Higher scorers rarely miss a deadline, feel guilty when they cancel plans, and take on extra responsibility because someone has to. Lower scorers are more selective — they commit when it genuinely matters and preserve flexibility for the rest. Both patterns serve social groups well when balanced with self-awareness.
