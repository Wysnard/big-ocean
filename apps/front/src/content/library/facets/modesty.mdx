## What modesty measures

Modesty captures the tendency to downplay your own achievements and deflect attention away from yourself. It reflects how comfortable you are with self-promotion versus how strongly you prefer to let your work speak for itself.

## When modesty is lower

People on the lower end own their strengths openly and are not shy about sharing their accomplishments when relevant. This is not arrogance — it is a straightforward relationship with their own competence. They understand that visibility matters and are comfortable advocating for themselves in professional and social settings.

## When modesty is higher

People on the higher end let their work speak for itself. They deflect praise, credit their team, and feel uncomfortable with spotlight attention. This humility earns quiet respect and keeps ego out of group dynamics, though it may sometimes lead to under-recognition in environments that reward self-promotion.

## How it shows up in daily life

Modesty influences how you respond to compliments, present your work, and negotiate for yourself. Higher scorers tend to say "it was a team effort" or redirect praise, while lower scorers tend to accept recognition and articulate their contributions clearly. In job interviews and salary negotiations, the difference can be consequential — not because either approach is wrong, but because different contexts reward different styles.
