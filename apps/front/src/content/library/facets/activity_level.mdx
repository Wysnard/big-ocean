## What activity level measures

Activity level captures your preferred pace of living — how much you fill your schedule, how quickly you move from one thing to the next, and how much physical and mental busyness feels comfortable rather than draining.

## When activity level is lower

People on the lower end move at a measured pace, savouring downtime and avoiding unnecessary rush. They prefer quality over quantity in their commitments and feel no pressure to fill every gap in the calendar. A slow morning or an unhurried weekend is not wasted time — it is time well spent.

## When activity level is higher

People on the higher end keep a full schedule and a fast pace. Sitting still too long makes them restless. They are drawn to multitasking, packed days, and physical movement. This energy makes them productive and hard to keep up with, though it can also make it difficult to slow down when rest is actually needed.

## How it shows up in daily life

Activity level is visible in how you structure your day and what you do with unscheduled time. Higher scorers tend to exercise regularly, say yes to more invitations, and feel uneasy during idle stretches. Lower scorers are more likely to protect downtime, enjoy leisurely activities, and feel energised by doing less rather than more. Neither pace is superior — they reflect different metabolisms of engagement.
