<h2 id="overview">Overview</h2>

The Compass is often the person people trust when the path is not obvious. This archetype mixes creative thinking with disciplined follow-through, but its signature quality is measured judgment. Rather than dominating a room, the Compass tends to orient people, reading competing priorities and helping a group move with less confusion.

<h2 id="strengths">Strengths</h2>

- You notice the tradeoffs in a situation before other people harden into positions.
- Your planning style is flexible enough to adapt while still protecting the core goal.
- Because you are not attached to constant attention, your input often lands with extra weight.
- You create systems that feel humane, not just efficient.

<h2 id="growth-areas">Growth areas</h2>

- Your care with decision-making can turn into delay when the moment actually calls for a clear bet.
- Others may mistake your restraint for lower conviction than you really feel.
- If you spend too much time translating between people, your own preferences can disappear from the room.
- You can become the unofficial stabilizer for everyone else, which is useful but tiring.

<h2 id="compatible-archetypes">Compatible archetypes</h2>

The Compass often works well with people who appreciate thoughtful pacing. It tends to click with visionary types who need grounding and gentle types who value psychological safety.
