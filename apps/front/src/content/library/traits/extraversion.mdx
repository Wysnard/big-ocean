## Scientific definition

Extraversion captures how strongly people orient toward social stimulation, outward expression, and positive activation. It includes warmth and sociability, but also assertiveness, energy level, and appetite for external engagement.

## Low extraversion in daily life

Lower extraversion often means solitude restores you, conversations go deeper than they go wide, and attention feels costly when it is constant. This range is not anti-social. It is usually selective about where social energy goes.

## Mid-range extraversion in daily life

Middle-range extraversion often appears as context sensitivity. You may enjoy hosting one night and disappear the next, shifting fluidly between visible engagement and private recovery.

## High extraversion in daily life

Higher extraversion tends to involve quick approach behavior, visible enthusiasm, and stronger reward from social contact. People in this range often create momentum in groups because action feels easier than hesitation.

## Facet breakdown

The six extraversion facets show whether the trait is being driven more by friendliness, assertiveness, activity, stimulation-seeking, or cheerful affect.
