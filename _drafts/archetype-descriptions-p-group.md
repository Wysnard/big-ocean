## PDE — Low O + High C + High E

### PDEW (Low O + High C + High E + High A)

Traditional authority with genuine heart — the kind of leader who earns trust not through charisma but through the accumulated weight of promises kept, standards maintained, and people genuinely cared for within those standards. Proven methods get loyalty because they've proven themselves; untested ideas get a fair hearing but they'd better bring evidence. The social confidence is real and warm, used to rally people around shared goals and clear expectations rather than personal glory.

The effect on a group is stabilizing and energizing simultaneously — people know exactly where they stand, what's expected, and that someone capable is looking out for them. The structures built here aren't cold or bureaucratic; they're designed with genuine awareness that real humans have to live inside them. The cost is a conservatism that can resist necessary change — the same commitment to what works can become a refusal to acknowledge when it's stopped working, and the warmth that makes the leadership feel personal can make any challenge to it feel like a personal betrayal.

**Notable figures:** Jacinda Ardern, Captain America (*Marvel*), Dwight D. Eisenhower
*"Plans are nothing; planning is everything."* — Eisenhower

### PDEN (Low O + High C + High E + Mid A)

Carries the standard for the group — visible, reliable, and diplomatically principled in a way that makes people feel represented rather than managed. The discipline is real but it's deployed through consensus rather than decree: expectations get set collaboratively, progress gets tracked transparently, and competing interests get negotiated with a fairness that earns authority rather than demanding it. Proven approaches form the foundation, but the diplomatic instinct ensures they're communicated in ways people can get behind.

There's a banner quality — something people rally around because it represents shared values clearly and consistently. Groups under this archetype's influence tend to be well-organized and genuinely aligned rather than just compliant. The cost is a caution that can become rigidity — the commitment to proven methods and diplomatic process can slow decision-making to a crawl, and the need for consensus can prevent the necessary unilateral action when the building is actually on fire.

**Notable figures:** Aragorn (*Lord of the Rings*), George Marshall, Sandra Day O'Connor
*"We don't accomplish anything in this world alone."* — Sandra Day O'Connor

### PDEC (Low O + High C + High E + Low A)

Shapes things through force of discipline and directness — proven methods, no shortcuts, and if the truth is uncomfortable, it's still getting said. Social confidence serves execution rather than popularity: the point of being in the room is to make things happen, not to make people feel good about things not happening. Standards are high, accountability is non-negotiable, and the track record justifies the intensity.

There's an anvil quality — hard, functional, and capable of shaping raw material into something useful through sustained, deliberate force. People who thrive under clear expectations and honest feedback do their best work here. The cost is a relentlessness that grinds: the bar doesn't lower, the directness doesn't soften, and the insistence on proven methods can become contempt for any approach that hasn't already been battle-tested. Loyalty is earned through competence, which means warmth is always conditional and the margin for error is thinner than most people can sustain.

**Notable figures:** Vince Lombardi, Minerva McGonagall (*Harry Potter*), Margaret Thatcher
*"Winning isn't everything, it's the only thing."* — Vince Lombardi

## PDA — Low O + High C + Mid E

### PDAW (Low O + High C + Mid E + High A)

The foundation stone — the kind of person whose dependable care is so structural that removing it would cause everything built on top of it to shift. Devotion expresses itself through preparation, consistency, and the quiet anticipation of what others will need before they know they need it. Practical to the core, but the practicality is in the service of love: the packed lunch with a note inside, the savings account started without being asked, the crisis plan that was already in place when the crisis arrived.

Close relationships carry an almost architectural reliability — load-bearing, designed for permanence, built to standard rather than to impress. The loyalty here is absolute and expressed through action rather than declaration. The cost is a rigidity in the role: the caretaker identity can become so central that there's no room left for personal needs, personal ambitions, or the messy, unstructured parts of being human that don't fit neatly into a plan made for someone else.

**Notable figures:** Marilla Cuthbert (*Anne of Green Gables*), Mr. Carson (*Downton Abbey*), Jimmy Carter
*"I have one life and one chance to make it count for something."* — Jimmy Carter

### PDAN (Low O + High C + Mid E + Mid A)

Weighs everything fairly and maintains balance through discipline — the kind of person who can be trusted to hold the scales steady regardless of who's standing on either side. Practical, measured, and reliable in a way that's more impressive the closer you look, because the consistency requires constant, quiet effort that never announces itself. Social engagement is purposeful and fair: opinions are considered, commitments are honored, and both sides of any argument get a genuine hearing before a decision gets made.

There's a scale quality — precise, balanced, and trusted precisely because it doesn't tip based on who's asking. People rely on this archetype for judgment calls because the track record speaks for itself. The cost is a measured quality that can calcify into predictability — the discipline that keeps things balanced can also keep them from ever tilting toward something passionate, spontaneous, or beautifully impractical. Being the reliable one is a valuable role, but it's a role, and the person underneath it sometimes goes unmet.

**Notable figures:** Ruth Bader Ginsburg, Captain Holt (*Brooklyn Nine-Nine*), Dwight Schrute (*The Office*)
*"Real change, enduring change, happens one step at a time."* — Ruth Bader Ginsburg

### PDAC (Low O + High C + Mid E + Low A)

Defends what works with a directness that doesn't negotiate — independent, disciplined, and uninterested in changing methods that have proven themselves just because something newer came along. The standards are set internally and maintained rigorously; external pressure to compromise gets acknowledged and then ignored. Social energy is invested in function, not warmth: collaboration happens when the task demands it, solitude resumes when it doesn't.

There's a rampart quality — built to last, built to protect, and not particularly interested in whether it looks inviting from the outside. People who share the commitment to proven methods and honest assessment find a deeply reliable ally here. The cost is a defensiveness that can tip into siege mentality — when everything outside the wall is treated as a potential threat to what works, good ideas and good people get turned away alongside the bad ones, and the fortress becomes less a choice than a habit.

**Notable figures:** Bear Grylls, Gloria Steinem, Javert (*Les Misérables*)
*"The truth will set you free, but first it will piss you off."* — Gloria Steinem

## PDI — Low O + High C + Low E

### PDIW (Low O + High C + Low E + High A)

Immovable foundation of quiet loyalty — the kind of person who supports everything above without needing to be seen, motivated by a devotion so deep it barely distinguishes itself from instinct. Practical, methodical, and utterly dependable, with a warmth that expresses itself through sustained action rather than words: showing up every time, exactly as promised, whether or not anyone is watching or keeping track. One-on-one depth over breadth, always.

The trust built here is geological — layer upon layer of consistent, reliable care that accumulates into something unshakeable. The people who depend on this archetype may not realize how much of their own stability is borrowed from it. The cost is an invisibility that runs deep: the support is so consistent it becomes expected rather than appreciated, the personal needs stay permanently subordinate to others', and the quiet warmth that powers the whole operation sometimes forgets that it deserves to be warmed in return.

**Notable figures:** Alfred Pennyworth (*Batman*), Laura Ingalls Wilder, George H.W. Bush
*"Actions speak louder than words."* — widely attributed (but this archetype's entire philosophy)

### PDIN (Low O + High C + Low E + Mid A)

Tracks direction with solitary precision — patient, fair, and methodical in a way that produces work of quiet, impeccable integrity. Decisions are weighed carefully against experience and principle, then executed without fanfare. Social energy is minimal and deliberate: small exchanges of genuine substance, zero tolerance for waste, and a fairness in judgment that people trust precisely because it's delivered without warmth or bias.

There's a sundial quality — steady, precise, indifferent to weather, measuring what matters with a reliability that technology can't replicate. People bring their hardest problems here because the assessment will be honest and the advice will be sound, even if neither is particularly comfortable. The cost is a solitary precision that can become its own prison — the methods that produce such excellent work are the same ones that keep the world small, the connections rare, and the life correct but underfed by the chaos and warmth that discipline can't provide.

**Notable figures:** Immanuel Kant, Mycroft Holmes (*Sherlock Holmes*), Janet Yellen
*"Two things fill the mind with ever new and increasing admiration and awe: the starry heavens above me and the moral law within me."* — Kant

### PDIC (Low O + High C + Low E + Low A)

Solitary lookout, disciplined guardian, answerable to no one — sees far, acts alone, and guards from a position of earned independence that has nothing to do with what anyone else thinks about the view. Proven methods and personal standards form the entire foundation; external validation is irrelevant noise. Work gets done with a methodical intensity that doesn't require company, conversation, or encouragement, and the quality speaks for itself.

There's a watchtower quality — elevated, isolated, and functionally essential in ways the people below rarely appreciate until the warning comes. The directness is absolute: observations are reported as facts, not opinions, and the response to disagreement is indifference rather than argument. The cost is a solitude so complete it can become a worldview — when competence is the only currency and independence is the only comfort, the result is a life that's impressively self-sufficient and strikingly empty of the mess and warmth that make most lives feel worth the effort.

**Notable figures:** Ayn Rand, Severus Snape (*Harry Potter*), Inspector Javert (*Les Misérables*)
*"The question isn't who is going to let me; it's who is going to stop me."* — Ayn Rand

## PBE — Low O + Mid C + High E

### PBEW (Low O + Mid C + High E + High A)

Practical warmth that gets things moving — stokes energy in others the way bellows feed a fire, not through dramatic inspiration but through grounded, genuine enthusiasm that makes people want to do their part. Friendly and straightforward, with a flexibility that cares more about results than about being right about the method. Social energy is generous and uncomplicated: real connection, no pretense, and an optimism rooted in experience rather than wishful thinking.

People get activated around this archetype — tasks that felt abstract become actionable, groups that felt scattered find their rhythm, and the warmth creates enough psychological safety for people to try things they'd normally avoid. The cost is a surface quality that can frustrate people who need depth — the practical warmth that keeps things moving may not slow down enough for the harder, quieter conversations, and the optimism that motivates others can sometimes look like avoidance of the things that aren't going well.

**Notable figures:** Ted Lasso (*Ted Lasso*), Magic Johnson, Ellen DeGeneres
*"Be curious, not judgmental."* — Ted Lasso (attributed to Walt Whitman in the show)

### PBEN (Low O + Mid C + High E + Mid A)

Where different paths meet — a practical social navigator who helps people find common ground without pretending the differences don't exist. Balanced and diplomatic, with a genuine interest in making things work for everyone rather than winning on behalf of any single side. The approach is practical rather than idealistic: compromise isn't a defeat, it's a tool, and the best solution is the one that actually gets implemented rather than the one that's theoretically perfect.

There's a crossroads quality — the place where different directions converge and decisions get made, facilitated by someone who can see all the routes and help others choose. Groups function better when this archetype is present because the navigation is practical and the diplomacy is real. The cost is a tendency to optimize for agreement rather than truth — the skill at finding common ground can become a habit of smoothing over tensions that actually needed to surface, and the practical focus on what works can sideline the question of what's right.

**Notable figures:** Kofi Annan, Hank Hill (*King of the Hill*), Nancy Pelosi
*"It is not necessary to change. Survival is not mandatory."* — W. Edwards Deming (the pragmatic spirit)

### PBEC (Low O + Mid C + High E + Low A)

Direct practical force — hits the nail, moves on, no wasted motion, no wasted words. The social energy is high but functional: the point is to get things done efficiently and honestly, not to win popularity contests or have interesting conversations about what might hypothetically work in theory. Proven approaches get trust; unproven approaches get skepticism delivered without a smile.

There's a hammer quality — simple, effective, and completely uninterested in being elegant about it. Problems get solved through the most direct route available, and the directness extends to every interaction. People who want honest, efficient collaboration thrive here; people who want nuance or emotional processing need to look elsewhere. The cost is a bluntness that mistakes efficiency for completeness — not every problem is a nail, but when the only language you speak is direct action, subtlety gets crushed alongside inefficiency, and the people who needed a conversation instead of a solution feel steamrolled rather than helped.

**Notable figures:** Judge Judy, Wolverine (*X-Men*), Mike Ehrmantraut (*Breaking Bad*)
*"Don't pee on my leg and tell me it's raining."* — Judge Judy

## PBA — Low O + Mid C + Mid E

### PBAW (Low O + Mid C + Mid E + High A)

Wraps practical reliability in warmth — the comfort of someone who shows up consistently, cares genuinely, and expresses both through familiar, steady patterns rather than dramatic gestures or novel approaches. Routines aren't boring here; they're the medium through which care gets communicated. The weekly call, the familiar recipe, the reliable presence at every event that matters — these aren't habits, they're devotion wearing comfortable clothes.

There's a quilt quality — made from familiar patterns, designed for warmth, and valued more with each passing year. People in this archetype's life feel wrapped in something reliable and soft, and that feeling of security is increasingly rare. The cost is a comfort that can resist growth — the familiar patterns that make everything feel safe can also make anything unfamiliar feel threatening, and the steady warmth that sustains relationships can also prevent them from evolving into something deeper, more honest, or more challenging than the original pattern allows.

**Notable figures:** Forrest Gump (*Forrest Gump*), Martha Stewart, Tom Hanks
*"Life is like a box of chocolates. You never know what you're gonna get."* — Forrest Gump

### PBAN (Low O + Mid C + Mid E + Mid A)

Finds the true horizontal — balanced, practical, and committed to keeping everything on an even plane regardless of what's trying to tilt it. Fairness is applied consistently and without drama; practical judgment keeps the approach grounded; and the moderate social energy means engagement is always proportional to what the situation actually requires. Not too much, not too little, reliably calibrated.

There's a level quality — the tool you trust when everything else looks crooked. People bring disputes, dilemmas, and difficult calls to this archetype because the assessment will be fair, practical, and unswayed by personality or politics. The cost is a levelness that can flatten: when everything gets evened out, the peaks disappear alongside the valleys, and the commitment to balance can become a resistance to anything that would disrupt the equilibrium — including the kinds of necessary disruptions that growth, passion, and genuine change require.

**Notable figures:** Mister Rogers, Atticus Finch (*To Kill a Mockingbird*), Charlie Munger
*"It's not always easy to do the right thing. But doing the right thing makes you strong."* — Mister Rogers (paraphrased)

### PBAC (Low O + Mid C + Mid E + Low A)

Marks their own ground and measures progress by their own standards — practical, self-reliant, and comfortable in their own skin in a way that doesn't need outside validation or even outside input. Familiar methods get loyalty because they've earned it; social engagement happens on their own terms; and the directness about all of it is so matter-of-fact that it barely registers as assertiveness — it's just honesty operating at its natural frequency.

There's a milestone quality — marking distance covered, placed deliberately, measuring progress against internal benchmarks rather than anyone else's expectations. People who value authenticity and self-sufficiency find this archetype deeply grounding; people who need encouragement or external processing find it distant. The cost is a self-containment that can become a closed loop — when all standards are internal and all progress is self-measured, there's no mechanism for discovering that the direction itself might need changing, and the independence that feels like freedom can also be a refusal to be influenced by anyone or anything.

**Notable figures:** Charles Bukowski, April Ludgate (*Parks and Recreation*), Coco Chanel
*"I don't care what you think about me. I don't think about you at all."* — Coco Chanel

## PBI — Low O + Mid C + Low E

### PBIW (Low O + Mid C + Low E + High A)

Deep practical care that surfaces when needed — like a wellspring, invisible most of the time but life-sustaining when tapped. The warmth isn't performed or broadcast; it rises quietly in response to actual need, offering exactly what's required with a practicality that makes the support immediately useful rather than merely emotional. Social energy is conserved for the people who matter most, and what they receive is a quality of care so dependable it becomes part of the architecture of their lives.

There's a wellspring quality — hidden, deep, and capable of sustaining far more than its quiet surface suggests. People draw from this archetype's reliability without always realizing they're doing it. The cost is a depletion that stays invisible: the care keeps flowing even when the source is running low, the needs of others always take priority, and the rich inner life stays so private that the people being sustained may never know how much it costs to keep the water flowing.

**Notable figures:** Mrs. Hudson (*Sherlock Holmes*), George Bailey (*It's a Wonderful Life*), Desmond Doss (*Hacksaw Ridge*)
*"Strange, isn't it? Each man's life touches so many other lives."* — Clarence, *It's a Wonderful Life*

### PBIN (Low O + Mid C + Low E + Mid A)

Navigates by fixed practical stars — reflective, fair-minded, and finding direction through a kind of quiet internal calibration that doesn't require consensus, conversation, or validation. Decisions are made carefully, with genuine fairness and a preference for proven approaches over experimental ones. Social energy is minimal but genuine: when engagement happens, it's deliberate, substantive, and free of pretense.

There's a sextant quality — a precise instrument that finds direction through observation rather than assertion, trusted because the readings are consistently accurate. People seek this archetype's judgment the way sailors check their instruments: not for comfort, but for truth. The cost is a life navigated with such careful measurement that the uncharted waters never get explored — the fixed stars provide reliable direction, but they only point to destinations that have already been imagined, and the map never expands beyond what the instrument can measure.

**Notable figures:** Hank Schrader (*Breaking Bad*), Elena Kagan, Marcus Aurelius

### PBIC (Low O + Mid C + Low E + Low A)

Stands alone, solid, unchanging — quiet practical authority that needs nothing from anyone and makes that independence look less like a choice than a natural law. Opinions are formed through experience, stated without qualification, and held without concern for whether they're popular. Social engagement is minimal and efficient: what needs saying gets said, what doesn't need saying doesn't get said, and the silence between is nobody's to fill.

There's a monolith quality — massive, still, and completely indifferent to the weather. People either respect the solidity or find it alienating; there's no middle ground because no middle ground is offered. The cost is an impregnability that prevents nourishment as effectively as it prevents assault — nothing gets in, which means nothing threatens, but nothing softens or surprises either. The life is consistent, self-determined, and honest, but it's also a closed system running on finite reserves of a fuel it refuses to replenish from outside.

## PFE — Low O + Low C + High E

### PFEW (Low O + Low C + High E + High A)

Warm and uncomplicated welcome — what you see is what you get, and what you get is someone who makes every room feel easier to be in simply by being genuinely, practically, generously present. No hidden agenda, no complicated subtext, no performance of anything other than honest enjoyment of the people and the moment. Social energy is high and freely given, connections form fast and feel real, and the preference for action over analysis means things happen around this archetype without ever feeling forced.

There's an open-door quality — literally and emotionally. People arrive and feel immediately at ease, not because the welcome was crafted but because it was instinctive. The shared experience is the currency: cooking together, road trips, spontaneous plans that become lasting memories. The cost is a simplicity that can avoid necessary complexity — the uncomplicated warmth that makes everything feel easy can also mean difficult conversations don't happen, difficult emotions don't get processed, and difficult truths don't get faced until they become crises that no amount of warmth can solve.

**Notable figures:** Kevin Hart, Andy Dwyer (*Parks and Recreation*), Rachael Ray
*"Everybody wants to be famous, but nobody wants to do the work."* — Kevin Hart

### PFEN (Low O + Low C + High E + Mid A)

Everyone gets a seat at the table — relaxed, practical, and diplomatically inclusive in a way that makes group dynamics feel easier without anyone noticing the facilitation happening. Social navigation comes naturally: tensions get deflected with a light touch, inclusion happens without fanfare, and the general atmosphere shifts toward "let's just figure this out together" rather than any particular ideology or agenda. Plans are flexible, methods are practical, and the goal is always what works for everyone rather than what's theoretically optimal.

There's a roundtable quality — democratic, accessible, and structured around the assumption that everyone has something useful to contribute. The diplomatic instinct here is practical rather than philosophical: not "everyone deserves a voice" in the abstract, but "we'll get a better answer if we hear from more people" in the concrete. The cost is a lack of edge — the inclusive flexibility that makes everything run smoothly can also mean nobody's vision dominates, nobody's standard wins, and the result is a comfortable median that nobody loves as much as they tolerate.

**Notable figures:** Mary Robinson, Leslie Knope (*Parks and Recreation*), Jamie Oliver

### PFEC (Low O + Low C + High E + Low A)

Raw practical energy with zero filter — burns through pretense at a speed that's either thrilling or alarming depending on where you're standing. Social confidence is high, structure is low, and the combination creates someone who says exactly what they see, does exactly what's needed, and moves on before the room has finished processing what just happened. There's no time for theory when reality is right there waiting to be dealt with.

There's a wildfire quality — consuming, clarifying, and impossible to control or predict. The pretense that survived everything else doesn't survive this. People who value honesty and action find the energy exhilarating; people who value process or diplomacy find it destructive. The cost is obvious and unrepentant: the same force that clears deadwood also scorches things that were still growing, the speed that's so effective in crisis is destabilizing in calm, and the directness that cuts through nonsense doesn't always stop at the nonsense.

## PFA — Low O + Low C + Mid E

### PFAW (Low O + Low C + Mid E + High A)

Quiet support structure for others to grow on — practical, gentle, and present in a way that doesn't need to direct or control, just provide the steady framework that something living can climb toward the light. The care is unglamorous and consistent: not the grand romantic gesture but the daily act of showing up, the practical help offered without being asked, the patience to let things develop at their own speed rather than the speed anxiety demands.

There's a trellis quality — functional, selfless, and designed entirely to help something else flourish. The warmth isn't dramatic but it's remarkably steady, and the people supported by it tend to achieve things they couldn't have achieved without that quiet scaffolding. The cost is a self-effacement that runs deep — the structure exists entirely in service of what grows on it, and when the growing thing no longer needs the support, the trellis is still there, still standing, but with no clear purpose of its own.

**Notable figures:** Beth March (*Little Women*), Uncle Iroh (*Avatar: The Last Airbender*), Joan Baez

Social engagement is easy and unfussy: people are welcome, conflict is navigated mildly, and the general approach is to reduce friction wherever it appears rather than investigate where it came from.

There's a clearing quality — empty in the best sense, defined not by what it contains but by the absence of anything that would prevent movement, growth, or rest. People near this archetype feel like they have room. The cost is an emptiness that can become genuine vacancy — when the entire identity is organized around making space for others, there may be nothing distinctly *there* when the others leave. Social engagement is selective and honest, warm when warranted and absent when it's not.

People who find this archetype's trail tend to be self-selecting: they wanted honest, no-nonsense company and they found it. Small, steady light in a quiet room — practical warmth offered sparingly but with a genuineness that more generous personalities can't match. The world is kept deliberately small, not from fear but from a clear understanding that the best care is concentrated care, given fully to the few who matter rather than diluted across everyone who asks. The cost is a smallness that can become rigid — the comfortable boundaries that protect the inner life can also prevent it from expanding, and the practical contentment that makes this archetype so restful can also be a quiet refusal to risk the discomfort that larger living requires.

### PFIC (Low O + Low C + Low E + Low A)

Solitary point of light, self-sufficient and needing nothing but its own sky — practical, independent, and completely at peace with a life stripped to its essentials. What's here was chosen deliberately; what's not here was deliberately excluded. Social engagement is minimal and honest: when it happens, it's direct and unvarnished; when it doesn't, there's no guilt or pretense about why. The solitude is a feature, the independence is the whole point, and the simplicity is the achievement.

There's a lone star quality — distant, self-sustaining, and visible precisely because nothing else is nearby to compete with it. People who encounter this archetype recognize a self-possession so thorough it borders on philosophical. The cost is a solitude that can become absolute — the self-sufficiency that looks like strength from the outside is genuinely sustainable until it isn't, and by the time the isolation becomes loneliness rather than freedom, the habits are so entrenched and the distance so established that reversing course feels impossible.

**Notable figures:** Christopher McCandless (*Into the Wild*), Greta Garbo, Matsuo Bashō
*"I want to be alone."* — Greta Garbo
