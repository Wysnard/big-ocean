## GDE — Mid O + High C + High E

### GDEW (Mid O + High C + High E + High A)

The person everyone quietly relies on and openly trusts — organized enough to keep a complex project on track, warm enough to make every person involved feel like they genuinely matter. Pragmatic to the core, but the pragmatism serves people, not just outcomes. New ideas get evaluated on whether they'll actually work, not whether they sound exciting, and that practical filter is what makes the things this archetype builds so durable.

Groups run better when this archetype is involved — not because they seize control, but because they create conditions where other people can do their best work without tripping over logistics or feeling unseen. The reliability is so consistent it can become invisible, like infrastructure nobody thanks until it breaks. The cost is exactly that: the emotional labor of holding everything together rarely gets acknowledged, and the warmth that powers the whole operation can quietly deplete when it only flows outward.

**Notable figures:** Queen Elizabeth II, Molly Weasley (*Harry Potter*), Sheryl Sandberg
*"I have to be seen to be believed."* — Queen Elizabeth II

### GDEN (Mid O + High C + High E + Mid A)

Steers without forcing — the kind of leader who keeps a group on course through pragmatic diplomacy rather than authority or volume. Organized enough to see the whole board, socially skilled enough to move pieces without anyone feeling like a pawn. Decisions get made through consultation and careful consensus, not decree, and the results hold up precisely because everyone involved felt heard during the process.

There's a steadiness here that groups instinctively orient around, especially in uncertainty. Plans stay realistic, expectations stay clear, and competing agendas get reconciled with a patience that never looks passive. The cost is a certain predictability — the diplomatic pragmatism that makes this archetype so effective also means they rarely surprise anyone, and the careful consensus-building can slow things down when what's actually needed is a fast, unilateral call that ruffles feathers.

**Notable figures:** Angela Merkel, Ned Stark (*Game of Thrones*), Colin Powell
*"Leadership is solving problems. The day soldiers stop bringing you their problems is the day you have stopped leading them."* — Colin Powell

---

### GDEC (Mid O + High C + High E + Low A)

Direct, organized, and impossible to ignore — the kind of person who tells you exactly what needs to happen, has already built the plan to make it happen, and doesn't particularly care whether the delivery was comfortable. Pragmatism runs the show: untested ideas get skepticism, proven methods get loyalty, and results matter more than feelings. The social confidence is real, but it serves execution, not popularity.

In any group, the effect is clarifying and occasionally blunt — ambiguity evaporates, accountability sharpens, and things that were drifting suddenly have direction and deadlines. People who want clear expectations and honest feedback thrive here; people who need encouragement before critique find it bruising. The cost is a rigidity that can look like stubbornness — the commitment to what works can become a refusal to consider what *might* work, and the directness that clears the air can also thin the ranks.

**Notable figures:** Simon Cowell, Colonel Tigh (*Battlestar Galactica*), Indra Nooyi
*"People ask the difference between a leader and a boss. The leader leads, and the boss drives."* — Indra Nooyi (paraphrased)

## GDA — Mid O + High C + Mid E

### GDAW (Mid O + High C + Mid E + High A)

The person who thought ahead so you didn't have to — organized not for the sake of order, but because anticipating what someone needs before they need it is the truest form of care they know. Pragmatic and devoted in equal measure, with a quiet warmth that shows up in action rather than words: the extra set of supplies packed, the schedule rearranged to accommodate someone's difficulty, the behind-the-scenes preparation that makes everything feel easier for everyone else.

Close relationships carry unusual reliability — the follow-through is absolute, the thoughtfulness is precise, and the loyalty is earned through thousands of small, consistent acts rather than grand declarations. This archetype is the foundation that other people build their lives on without always realizing it. The cost is a tendency to define self-worth through service to others — when the caretaking goes unreciprocated or unacknowledged, resentment can build silently beneath a surface that never stops smiling and never stops working.

**Notable figures:** Dr. Watson (*Sherlock Holmes*), Joanna Gaines, Mary Berry
*"If a job's worth doing, it's worth doing well."* — Mary Berry

### GDAN (Mid O + High C + Mid E + Mid A)

The piece that holds everything together without drawing attention to itself — pragmatic, balanced, and quietly essential to every structure that depends on them. Decisions get made carefully, with genuine fairness and a preference for solutions that serve the whole rather than any single interest. Social engagement is selective and purposeful: meaningful contribution over constant presence, quality input over quantity of interaction.

There's a keystone quality here — remove this archetype from a team, a family, or a project and the whole thing shifts in ways nobody anticipated. The reliability is so deeply woven into the fabric that it becomes expected rather than appreciated. The cost is precisely that invisibility: being essential and being recognized are two different things, and this archetype tends to get far more of the former than the latter. The steady pragmatism that others depend on can also feel like a trap — always the responsible one, rarely the spontaneous one.

**Notable figures:** Tim Duncan, Condoleezza Rice, Warren Buffett
*"The stock market is a device for transferring money from the impatient to the patient."* — Warren Buffett

### GDAC (Mid O + High C + Mid E + Low A)

Pragmatic, organized, and unapologetically independent — the kind of person who builds strong systems and defends them with a directness that leaves no ambiguity about where the line is. New ideas are welcome only when they can prove they outperform what's already working; enthusiasm alone doesn't open this door. Social engagement is purposeful: contribute, collaborate where it matters, then return to the work without lingering over small talk.

There's a fortress quality — reliable, well-constructed, and not easily moved by external pressure or popular opinion. People know exactly what to expect and exactly what the standards are. The cost is an inflexibility that can look like closed-mindedness — the defenses that protect quality and consistency can also keep out ideas and people that deserved a longer hearing than they got, and the directness that keeps things efficient can keep them cold.

**Notable figures:** Jack Nicholson, Stannis Baratheon (*Game of Thrones*), Rosa Parks
*"I have learned over the years that when one's mind is made up, this diminishes fear."* — Rosa Parks

## GDI — Mid O + High C + Low E

### GDIW (Mid O + High C + Low E + High A)

Invisible and indispensable — the kind of person whose careful, behind-the-scenes work holds everything up, motivated not by recognition but by genuine devotion to the people who depend on it. Organization is an act of love here: the meticulously prepared meal, the perfectly maintained home, the quietly managed crisis that nobody else even knew was happening. Pragmatic to the bone, but the pragmatism is in service of people, not productivity.

Close relationships carry extraordinary depth and reliability. Promises aren't made lightly and they aren't broken. The loyalty expressed through sustained action — showing up, every time, exactly as needed — creates a trust that words alone could never build. The cost is a quiet self-erasure: so much energy goes into supporting others that personal needs get perpetually deferred, and the rich inner life stays unexpressed because there's always someone else who needs attending to first.

**Notable figures:** Clara Barton, George Harrison, Florence Nightingale
*"If I can help somebody as I pass along, then my living shall not be in vain."* — Mahalia Jackson (the spirit)

### GDIN (Mid O + High C + Low E + Mid A)

Measures twice, cuts once — and the cut is so clean it barely needs explaining. Methodical, fair-minded, and deeply private, with a quiet precision that produces work of unusual integrity. Decisions get weighed from every angle before being made, and once they're made, the follow-through is absolute. Social energy is conserved and invested carefully: small groups, substantive exchanges, zero tolerance for gossip or politics.

There's a meridian quality — perfectly calibrated, finding the true line through noise and bias with a consistency that borders on uncanny. People may not seek this archetype's company for fun, but they seek it for judgment. When something important needs to be done right, this is who gets the call. The cost is a life that can become too measured — the precision that produces excellent work can also squeeze out spontaneity, play, and the kind of beautiful mess that happens when you let go of the ruler for five minutes.

**Notable figures:** George Washington, Spock (*Star Trek*), Jane Goodall
*"The least I can do is speak out for those who cannot speak for themselves."* — Jane Goodall

### GDIC (Mid O + High C + Low E + Low A)

Solitary precision as a way of life — shapes raw material into something functional and exact, whether that material is code, wood, ideas, or systems. Independent, exacting, and unbothered by whether anyone approves of the methods or the personality. What matters is whether the work holds up to scrutiny, and it almost always does, because the standards are set internally and they don't bend for comfort or consensus.

There's a chisel quality — every stroke intentional, nothing wasted, nothing decorative unless it's also structural. People encounter the output more often than the person, and that's by design. The cost is a starkness that can shade into isolation — the door isn't just closed, it's locked, and the directness that keeps the work honest can keep the relationships sparse. The few connections that exist are built on mutual respect for competence, not warmth, and that's a narrower foundation than most people can build a life on.

**Notable figures:** Nikola Tesla, Ridley Scott, Hedy Lamarr
*"If I'd listened to what people told me, I'd still be doing Alien sequels."* — Ridley Scott (paraphrased)

## GBE — Mid O + Mid C + High E

### GBEW (Mid O + Mid C + High E + High A)

The human equivalent of a room that's always the right temperature — grounded warmth that makes groups feel whole without anyone quite understanding what changed when this archetype walked in. People get woven together naturally, not through organized effort but through a social instinct that notices who's on the outside and brings them in, who's carrying something heavy and offers to share the load. Adaptable without being inconsistent, practical without being cold, present without being overwhelming.

The effect on any gathering is subtle and profound: conversations deepen, tension softens, and people who might never have spoken find themselves connected. There's a loom-like quality — taking separate threads and turning them into something that holds together. The cost is a self-forgetting: so attuned to the emotional temperature of the room that personal needs get quietly shelved, and the warmth that everyone depends on has to come from somewhere, even when the well is running lower than anyone notices.

**Notable figures:** Desmond Tutu, Maya Angelou, Dolly Parton
*"If you see someone without a smile, give 'em yours."* — Dolly Parton

### GBEN (Mid O + Mid C + High E + Mid A)

A safe port in any storm — grounded, adaptive, and diplomatically welcoming in a way that makes even the most anxious person feel like things will probably be fine. Pragmatic enough to deal with what's actually happening rather than what should be happening, socially skilled enough to navigate competing interests without creating new ones. When groups fracture or tensions escalate, this archetype naturally becomes the place where different sides come to find common ground.

There's a harbor quality — sheltered, stable, and open to whoever needs it. People arrive stressed and leave calmer, not because the problems were solved but because the presence itself was steadying. The cost is that being the safe port means never being the one who gets to arrive somewhere and be held — the role becomes the identity, the steadiness becomes the expectation, and the person underneath the diplomat rarely gets asked what *they* need.

**Notable figures:** Pope Francis, Mr. Miyagi (*The Karate Kid*), Fred Rogers
*"Won't you be my neighbor?"* — Fred Rogers

### GBEC (Mid O + Mid C + High E + Low A)

Honest, present, and refreshingly uncomplicated — strikes sparks in conversation through directness rather than charm, says what needs saying without wrapping it in three layers of diplomacy, and moves on without lingering over whether it landed perfectly. Grounded enough that the honesty feels reliable rather than reckless, social enough that it gets delivered with energy rather than coldness. What you see is exactly what you get, and what you get is someone who values real over nice.

The effect on a group is polarizing in the best sense — BS dissipates, pretense drops, and the people who stay discover they prefer the room this way. Conversations cut to what matters faster. The cost is a bluntness that doesn't always account for timing — being right and being kind aren't the same thing, and the speed at which truth gets delivered can outpace the listener's readiness to hear it. Not everyone wants the flint struck that close to their face.

**Notable figures:** Pink, Bronn (*Game of Thrones*), Charles Barkley
*"I may be wrong, but I doubt it."* — Charles Barkley

## GBA — Mid O + Mid C + Mid E

### GBAW (Mid O + Mid C + Mid E + High A)

Quiet, steady nourishment — the kind of presence that rises naturally to meet whatever's needed without drama, urgency, or expectation of credit. Caring expresses itself practically: the meal left at the door, the problem solved before it was fully articulated, the kind of consistent attention that makes people feel held without feeling managed. Social energy flows toward close relationships and small settings where the warmth doesn't have to compete with noise.

There's a spring-like quality — refreshment offered without fanfare, available to whoever approaches, never running dry because the source is deep and self-renewing. People in this archetype's orbit feel calmer and more capable, often without identifying why. The cost is a quietness that can shade into passivity — the care is always available but rarely asserted, which means louder needs and louder people tend to set the agenda while this archetype's own preferences go perpetually unvoiced.

**Notable figures:** Keanu Reeves, Princess Diana, Mary Poppins (*P.L. Travers*)
*"Carry out a random act of kindness, with no expectation of reward."* — Princess Diana

### GBAN (Mid O + Mid C + Mid E + Mid A)

Remarkable balance across every dimension — the ability to sit with ambiguity without rushing to resolve it, hold opposing viewpoints without needing to pick a winner, and adapt to almost anything without losing their center. The equilibrium isn't passive; it's the rarest kind of active work, recalibrating constantly so that nothing tips too far in any direction. Grounded pragmatism keeps the approach realistic, diplomatic instinct keeps the interactions smooth, and neither the quiet nights nor the social ones feel like a compromise.

The effect is stabilizing in a way that's hard to articulate — people feel more grounded just from proximity, as if the equilibrium is slightly contagious. Conflict loses its edge when this archetype is present, not because it's suppressed but because it gets reframed into something manageable. The cost is an opacity: when every perspective gets equal consideration and every situation gets a measured response, it can become genuinely unclear what this person's own desires, passions, and unfiltered opinions actually are — even to themselves.

**Notable figures:** Dalai Lama, Albus Dumbledore (*Harry Potter*), Ban Ki-moon
*"Be kind whenever possible. It is always possible."* — Dalai Lama

### GBAC (Mid O + Mid C + Mid E + Low A)

Solid, self-contained, and immovable by outside pressure — grounded enough to stay planted, independent enough to not care whether anyone else is planted beside them. Opinions are held with quiet conviction, formed through practical experience rather than theory, and delivered without the diplomatic cushion that most people expect. Social engagement happens when it's useful or interesting and doesn't happen when it's not, with zero energy wasted on guilt or performance.

There's a granite quality — what's there is what's there, and no amount of pressure changes the composition. People who value straightforwardness and self-sufficiency find this archetype deeply reliable; people who need warmth or validation find it unyielding. The cost is a self-sufficiency so thorough that it can become a wall — the independence that feels like strength from the inside can read as indifference from the outside, and the people who might have gotten closer never find a door that looks like it was meant to be opened.

**Notable figures:** Tommy Lee Jones, Ron Swanson (*Parks and Recreation*), Tina Turner
*"I know what I'm about, son."* — Ron Swanson

## GBI — Mid O + Mid C + Low E

### GBIW (Mid O + Mid C + Low E + High A)

Quiet nourishment that most people don't notice until they realize they've been depending on it — steady, grounded care offered without fanfare, without agenda, and without any need for the caring to be visible or acknowledged. Pragmatic and patient, with an inner warmth that expresses itself through small, reliable acts rather than grand emotional displays. Social energy is limited and directed almost exclusively toward the people who matter most.

There's a spring quality — hidden, deep, and life-sustaining, discovered only by those who know where to look. Relationships built with this archetype carry a simplicity that more complicated personalities find restful. The cost is an invisibility that can become genuine loneliness — the care flows so quietly that it barely registers as care, the inner world stays so private that even close friends may not realize how much is happening beneath the calm, steady surface.

Calm surface, deep processing underneath — the kind of person who absorbs everything, weighs it with genuine fairness, and surfaces with an assessment that's more useful than anything the louder participants produced. Grounded and reflective, with a balanced temperament that avoids both the impulse to react and the paralysis of overthinking. Small settings and substantive conversations are the preferred operating environment; anything else is tolerable but not nourishing.

There's a still-water quality — undisturbed, deep, and clearer than anything turbulent. People who spend time with this archetype tend to slow down and think better, as if the stillness is gently contagious. The cost is a containment that can become constriction — the calm that others find so restful can also mean emotions stay submerged longer than is healthy, and the fairness extended to every external perspective doesn't always get extended inward.

### GBIC (Mid O + Mid C + Low E + Low A)

Solid, quiet, and completely self-contained — the kind of presence that doesn't need external validation, external input, or external company to feel complete. Opinions are formed through careful observation and practical experience, then stated without apology or qualification. The independence is total: no need to explain, no need to justify, no need to belong to anything that doesn't serve a clear purpose.

There's a geological quality — formed slowly, resistant to erosion, unbothered by weather. The cost is a solitude that hardens over time — what began as a preference becomes a fortress, and the directness that kept things honest in small doses can become an effective deterrent to anyone trying to get close.

## GFE — Mid O + Low C + High E

### GFEW (Mid O + Low C + High E + High A)

Relaxed warmth that makes everyone comfortable — the room genuinely feels better when this archetype walks in, not because of anything dramatic but because the ease is so authentic that other people's shoulders drop three inches just from proximity. Social energy is generous and uncomplicated, offered without agenda or performance. Plans are loose, expectations are low, and the resulting atmosphere gives everyone permission to simply be themselves.

There's a campfire quality — warm, inviting, naturally gathering people into a circle without anyone organizing it. Conversations happen easily, laughter comes quickly, and strangers become friends with less effort than it usually takes. The cost is a casualness that can frustrate people who need more structure, follow-through, or intensity — the warmth is consistent but the commitment is flexible, and the things that needed to get done sometimes don't, because the hangout was just that good.

**Notable figures:** Jack Johnson (the musician), Hagrid (*Harry Potter*), Guy Fieri
*"The best surfer out there is the one having the most fun."* — attributed to various, embodied by Jack Johnson

### GFEN (Mid O + Low C + High E + Mid A)

Relaxed social navigation — moves through groups and situations with a practical ease, reading the room without appearing to try, adjusting course without appearing to calculate. Diplomatic instinct is natural, not studied: tensions get diffused through casual redirects, competing interests get managed with a light touch, and the whole thing looks like someone who's just easygoing rather than someone who's actively keeping the peace.

There's a waypoint quality — a reliable marker that people use to orient themselves, even if they don't realize it. Not the destination, but the thing that keeps everyone from getting lost along the way. The cost is an absence of urgency that can look like absence of investment — the relaxed approach that makes everything smoother can also make it hard to tell what actually matters to this person, and the diplomatic flexibility can shade into a willingness to go wherever the current takes them, including away from things that deserved more commitment.

**Notable figures:** Bill Murray, Jimmy Buffett, Owen Wilson

### GFEC (Mid O + Low C + High E + Low A)

Sudden practical energy — arrives, speaks plainly, gets things moving, and doesn't linger over whether the delivery was polished. Grounded and spontaneous in a combination that makes this archetype surprisingly effective in chaos: no need for a plan, no need for consensus, just clear-eyed assessment and immediate action. Social confidence is high but it's functional, not performative — the point is getting things done, not getting liked.

There's a squall quality — brief, powerful, and clearing the air whether you asked for it or not. Rooms get unstuck, decisions get made, and the energy moves on as quickly as it arrived. The cost is a bluntness that leaves marks — speaking plainly in the moment is efficient, but the people on the receiving end don't always process at the same speed, and the wake of a squall sometimes includes damage that wasn't part of the plan.

## GFA — Mid O + Low C + Mid E

### GFAW (Mid O + Low C + Mid E + High A)

Gentle, practical, and quietly tending to what grows — whether that's a garden, a relationship, or a small corner of the world that most people walk past without noticing. The care is unhurried and unglamorous: showing up consistently, offering what's needed without overcomplicating it, creating the conditions for growth and then trusting the process. Social energy flows at a natural rhythm — neither seeking company nor avoiding it, just being present when present makes sense.

There's a brook quality — gentle, steady, following its own path without forcing anything to change. People near this archetype feel tended without feeling managed, which is a distinction most caregivers struggle with. The cost is a smallness that can become constriction — the contentment with a quiet scope can also mean bigger ambitions never get tested, and the gentle patience that makes this archetype so nurturing can also be a way of avoiding the discomfort that growth requires.

**Notable figures:** Beatrix Potter, Neville Longbottom (*Harry Potter*), Bob Ross

There's a ridgeline quality — elevated, independent, and offering a clear line of sight that most people can't access because they're too caught up in the terrain below. The cost is a detachment that can read as disregard — following your own path is admirable until it means consistently walking past people who needed you to stop, and the directness that keeps things honest can also keep them lonely.

### GFIW (Mid O + Low C + Low E + High A)

Practical enough to handle what needs handling, flexible enough to adapt when life insists, and fair-minded enough to consider perspectives without drama. ### GFIC (Mid O + Low C + Low E + Low A)

A solitary marker on a quiet path — self-built, practical, and standing alone by choice rather than circumstance. Life is stripped to what's essential and what's honest, with zero ornamentation or social performance. Opinions are plainly stated, help is offered practically when asked and withheld without guilt when it's not, and the solitude is a feature, not a bug. What's here was put here deliberately, and what's not here was deliberately left out.

There's a cairn quality — minimal, functional, placed with intention. People encounter it and find it useful; nobody expects it to be warm. The cost is a bareness that can become starkness — the simplicity that makes this archetype's life so clean can also strip away the things that make life rich, and the independence that stands so firmly alone can forget that some loads are lighter when shared.

**Notable figures:** Edward Abbey, Agnes Martin, The Man with No Name (*Clint Eastwood westerns*)
